## City: Portsmouth, Hampshire

Portsmouth is the only **island city** in the UK and the densest local authority outside London. Commercial property here is shaped by three forces: the **Royal Navy / BAE Systems defence cluster** at Portsmouth Naval Base and the Portsmouth shipbuilding yards, the **leisure-led Gunwharf Quays / Spinnaker Tower / Southsea seafront** axis, and the **two-university student footprint** (University of Portsmouth + Solent feeder) that drives a substantial HMO and student-led F&B economy. The market sits across the Solent from Southampton and shares the M27 logistics corridor — but Portsmouth itself reads quite differently to its sister city: less retail, more leisure, more naval and more HMO conversion.

### Demand-side signals

- **Largest employers / job clusters:** Royal Navy / Portsmouth Naval Base (3,500+ uniformed + civilian), BAE Systems Maritime Services (1,500+ at Portsmouth shipyard), Portsmouth City Council, Queen Alexandra Hospital (NHS Portsmouth Hospitals University NHS Trust), University of Portsmouth (3,000+ staff), IBM legacy at Lakeside (now multi-tenant), Wiggle / Chain Reaction Cycles (HQ).
- **University presence:** University of Portsmouth c. 28,000 students; high HMO demand → most active HMO-conversion city in the south.
- **Tourism profile:** Strong — Spinnaker Tower, HMS Victory, Mary Rose, Gunwharf Quays outlet shopping, Southsea seafront. Drives hotel and Airbnb mortgage demand.
- **NHS trust footprint:** Queen Alexandra Hospital is the dominant healthcare anchor; care-home cluster along Cosham/Drayton corridor.
- **Population + working-age trend:** Stable population c. 210K; student in-flow positive.

### Cross-Solent / regional context

Portsmouth pairs with Southampton on the M27 industrial-warehouse market — both city sites should reference the **Solent corridor** view on industrial property-type pages. The **defence cluster (Portsmouth-led)** and the **port / cruise cluster (Southampton-led)** are distinct economic stories but share underwriting characteristics for owner-occupier business mortgages — long-term covenants, stable employment, public-sector-adjacent tenants — that lenders price favourably.
